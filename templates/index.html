Plain English Guide
Hashing
Hashing scrambles your password into a random-looking string that cannot be reversed. Even if someone sees the scrambled version, they cannot figure out your original password from it.
Entropy
A math score that measures how random and unpredictable your password is. The higher the number, the more combinations an attacker would have to try. Anything above 60 bits is considered strong.
k-Anonymity
A privacy trick that lets us check if your password appears in a breach database without ever showing the real password. Only a 5-character snippet of the scrambled version is shared. The actual match is done on your own device.
Breach Database
A massive list of passwords that have been stolen in real-world hacks and data leaks. If your password is on this list, attackers already know about it and could use it to break into your accounts.
Crack Time
An estimate of how long it would take a powerful computer to guess your password by trying every possible combination. Longer is much, much better. A few seconds means your password is not safe at all.
Percentile
How your password compares to all other passwords people commonly use. Being at 90% means your password is stronger than 90 out of every 100 passwords. Higher is better.
